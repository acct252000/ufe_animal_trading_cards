Narwhal

The narwhal has a large tusk which is actually a protruding canine tooth.

  • Scientific Name: Monodon monoceros
  • Average Weight: 1,800 to 3,500 lbs
  • Habitat: Canadian Artic, Icelandic and Russian Waters
  • Main Diet: Flatfish

The narwhal is a medium sized tooth whale closely related to the Beluga whale. Males are slightly larger than females. Narwhals communicate with clicks, whistles, and knocks. Narwhals can live up to 50 years. They are often killed by suffocation when the sea ice freezes over. Another cause of fatality, specifically among young whales, is starvation. The current population of the narwhal is about 75,000.
